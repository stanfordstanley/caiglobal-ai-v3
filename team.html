Stanley X.L. Diao

Co-Founder, CAI Global

Stanley X.L. Diao is a cross-border AI ecosystem builder and venture strategist focused on connecting North America's AI innovation capacity with Asia's capital, institutions, and emerging technology markets. He is the Co-Founder of CAI (Canada–Asia Council for AI & Digital Innovation), a platform advancing AI governance, venture creation, and global technology collaboration.

Since 2017, Stanley has worked across venture investing, accelerator building, and technology commercialization through the Draper Dragon and DFJ ecosystem, supporting founder-led companies across AI, deep technology, Web3, and digital infrastructure. He currently leads strategic partnerships at the Cyberport × Draper Dragon Joint Accelerator, creating pathways between startups, investors, and innovation ecosystems across Canada, Silicon Valley and Asia.

Previously, Stanley worked in Big Four at KPMG deal advisory/PwC, supporting cross-border transactions and strategic projects exceeding US$1.8 billion in aggregate value, with earlier strategy consulting experience at BCG/Monitor.

Stanley holds a Master's degree in Philosophy, Politics & Economics from Shanghai University of Finance and Economics, completed Stanford Graduate School of Business' Ignite program, Harvard Kennedy School executive education, and AI training at the Vector Institute.

Jie Shen

Co-Founder, CAI Bio Venture Studio

Jie Shen is a healthcare and life sciences ecosystem builder focused on supporting AI-enabled biotechnology companies, emerging healthcare ventures, and global innovation platforms. As Co-Founder of CAI Bio Venture Studio, she leads life sciences venture building initiatives at the intersection of AI, biotechnology, and translational healthcare.

Jie has advised founders, investors, and CEOs of multinational corporations and high-growth companies on leadership strategy, organizational scaling, and global expansion. Through her work as a Senior Partner at a global executive research firm, where she co-leads Healthcare, Life Sciences, and Technology practices, she has helped build leadership teams for innovative companies across multiple markets, including AI-driven drug discovery and biotech platforms.

Before entering executive advisory, Jie worked as a research scientist, developing expertise in microbiology and pharmaceutical sciences. She holds a Master of Science degree in Microbiology and Pharmaceutical Science from the China State Institute of Pharmaceutical Industry, an MBA from the University of Illinois, and completed the Organizational Leadership and Entrepreneurship Certificate at Harvard Business School.

Her work spans the intersection of scientific innovation, capital deployment, and leadership development for the next generation of healthcare companies.
